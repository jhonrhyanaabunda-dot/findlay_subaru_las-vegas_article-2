01The dealership

Findlay, since 1961.

Pete Findlay opened a Las Vegas dealership in 1961, a year when the Strip had eleven hotels and the city had not yet hit a quarter-million people.

He sold cars the way he was raised to sell anything: shake the hand, mean it, do the deed. His son Cliff joined him in 1970 at twenty-two years old, ran the store as GM by 1978, and was running the company by 1979. Cliff is President now. Sixty-five years in.

The group is thirty-five dealerships across Nevada, Arizona, Idaho, Washington, and Utah today, and ranked #21 on the 2024 Automotive News Top 150. It is a family business at the scale of a regional chain, which is a hard thing to stay. Most don't.

Findlay Subaru of Las Vegas opened its current home at 6455 Roy Horn Way in September 2017, an $8.67 million, two-story, 65,501-square-foot building designed around the experience instead of around the inventory. Real lounge. Kids' area. Belle the golden retriever, who belongs to Fixed Operations Director Carmine Kopecky and holds court near the service drive most days.

03The math

How financing actually works.

The number on the desk is the number in the finance office.

That sounds like the absolute floor of a dealership's job, and it is, and most of them still don't do it.

What you'll see, every time

  • Negotiated vehicle price
  • $499 documentation fee
  • Nevada sales tax, 6.85% state plus Clark County local, 8.375% combined
  • DMV registration
  • Optional dealer-installed accessories (only what you ask for)

That is it. The doc fee does not change between the desk and F&I. Average dealer add-ons across our recent transactions run roughly $149, against a Las Vegas market where $400 to $800 is standard. Our pricing earned a CarEdge Transparency Grade A (100/100).

Pre-approval, ten minutes

Pre-approval lives at express.subaruoflasvegas.com, runs on a soft credit pull (no FICO impact), and takes about ten minutes. The Finance team works a multi-lender network plus Subaru Motors Finance and matches the deal to the credit profile, not the other way around. Tier-1 buyers get the SMF promotional rates with approved credit. Tier-2 and Tier-3 (650–719) goes to mainstream lenders. Subprime and first-time buyers go to dedicated programs.

Programs worth knowing about

  • Subaru Trade Up Advantage: factory loyalty incentive on Subaru-to-Subaru trades.
  • Subaru Love-Encore: $500 credit at lease-end for returning Subaru Motors Finance customers.
  • First-time buyer program: SMF financing for limited credit history, with income and co-signer documentation.
  • EV incentive routing: federal tax credit guidance for qualified Solterra buyers.

Approved-credit note: financing terms depend on creditworthiness, and Subaru Motors Finance promotional programs require approved credit.

04The trade

Trading your current vehicle.

Twenty to thirty minutes.

The team led by Used Car Sales Manager Chad Saunders (ten years at Findlay, twenty-four in the Subaru brand) inspects the vehicle, pulls live Black Book and KBB Instant Cash Offer numbers, and writes the appraisal. If a Carvana offer comes back higher, we say so out loud.

The structural reason to trade rather than sell privately is Nevada's tax-on-the-difference rule. The state taxes you on the new-vehicle price minus the trade allowance, which on most deals saves $1,500 to $3,000. Returning Subaru drivers stack the Trade Up Advantage incentive on top.

07Questions people actually ask

Frequently asked questions about buying a Subaru in Las Vegas.

Why buy here instead of Centennial Subaru?

Different sides of town, mostly. We are off the I-215 in the southwest valley, ten minutes from Henderson and Summerlin. Centennial is in north Las Vegas. Pick whichever is closer.

The substantive difference is the Stress Free Zone: the out-the-door price in writing, the $499 doc fee, the same person from hello to keys. The awards back that up: Subaru's 2023 Retailer of the Year, the 2026 HEART Award, the J.D. Power 2024 Dealer of Excellence, and six straight Subaru Customer Commitment Awards from 2018 to 2023.

How long has Findlay sold Subaru in Las Vegas?

The current store opened in September 2017 at 6455 Roy Horn Way. Findlay Subaru previously operated at 5385 W. Sahara Avenue.

The parent group has been a Nevada operator since 1961.

What is the Stress Free Zone?

A car-buying experience without theater. No pressure. The out-the-door price in writing on the first conversation. The same consultant from hello to keys.

How does the trade-in process work?

Bring the vehicle in. Twenty to thirty minutes. The team writes a Black Book and KBB Instant Cash Offer-based appraisal led by Chad Saunders.

Nevada taxes the trade difference, not the full new-car price, saving most buyers $1,500 to $3,000 versus a private sale. Online appraisals are at subaruoflasvegas.com/trade-appraisal.
